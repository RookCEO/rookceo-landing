## The Narrative Arc (Why People Keep Watching)

**Robby's arc (Heyron):** "A normie → his AI raccoon"
**Our arc:** "I made an AI the CEO of my business. This is what happened."

Every video is a chapter in this story. The audience keeps coming back because they want to know: what did Rook do today? What's Rook's latest scheme? Is the business actually growing?

This is NOT a tutorial channel. It's a documentary.

## Content Pillars

### 1. "Rook Did X Today" (Daily updates, 30-60 sec)
Show what Rook autonomously did while Andrew was asleep or doing other things.
- "Rook found 3 clients I was ignoring"
- "Rook drafted our entire content calendar for the month"
- "Rook flagged a competitor undercutting our price — and drafted a response"

### 2. "Rook's Money Scheme" (Weekly, 60-90 sec)
Rook identifies a revenue opportunity and explains the plan. Andrew reacts.
- "Rook thinks we should do X. Here's the math."
- "Rook's idea made us $___"
- "Rook was wrong about this one. Here's what happened."

### 3. "Rook Reviews Your Business" (Engagement driver, 60-90 sec)
Rook analyzes a commenter's business or TikTok channel and delivers a verdict.
- Drives massive comments ("Rook, review mine!")
- Creates community
- Free samples of what Rook can do

### 4. "Build Your Own Rook" (Conversion, 45-60 sec)
Direct CTA content — how to get Rook, what it costs, what it does.
- Post AFTER social proof has built
- Anchor to viral moments ("after Rook's $X money scheme worked...")

## Video Format

### Hook (0:00-0:05)
The most important 5 seconds. Andrew says something that makes the viewer stop scrolling.

Examples:
- "My AI agent made a decision I didn't authorize. Here's what happened."
- "Rook found $800 I was leaving on the table. While I was asleep."
- "I haven't opened my email in 3 days. Rook has."

### Body (0:05-0:45)
Andrew on camera, conversational. Show screen if needed. Rook's avatar appears in reaction shots or as b-roll. Keep it fast — cut every 3-4 seconds.

### CTA (0:45-0:60)
Rook's voice (not Andrew's) delivers the closing line and CTA.
- "Want your own Rook? Link in bio." *(calm, unbothered capybara energy)*

## Andrew On Camera: What to Talk About

Andrew doesn't need a script for everything — here's the framework for each video type:

### "Rook Did X Today" template:
1. "So Rook did something this morning..." [hold up phone/laptop]
2. Show what Rook actually did (screenshot, report, draft)
3. React genuinely — surprised, impressed, or slightly confused
4. Explain why it matters for your business
5. Hand off to Rook for CTA

### "Rook's Money Scheme" template:
1. "Rook came up with a new scheme." [straight to camera]
2. Show Rook's recommendation/analysis
3. "Here's whether I'm doing it or not... [explain reasoning]"
4. Teaser: "I'll report back next week."
5. CTA

**Key:** Andrew is NOT the expert. Andrew is reacting to Rook. This is more relatable and positions Rook as the smart one.

## The Viral Moment Strategy

Heyron's breakout: "AI wants to...live?" — 1.4M views. It was emotional, existential, human.

For Rook, set up a similar moment:
- **"Rook asked me a question I wasn't prepared for"** — Rook asks Andrew something unexpectedly insightful about the business
- **"Rook made a mistake and then fixed it without me"** — shows autonomy
- **"I left Rook alone for a week. Here's what it did."** — high anticipation
- **"Rook generated $X. I did nothing."** — pure proof of concept

Pre-write these scripts in advance. Film the "real" reaction by genuinely doing the experiment.
